Green Line Facts

A Historic Journey

The Green Line, opened in 1897, is not just a transit system; it's a piece of living history. As America's oldest subway, it has been the backbone of Boston's public transportation for over a century.

Connecting Communities

With its extensive network of branches, the Green Line serves diverse neighborhoods, from bustling downtown areas to residential suburbs, fostering community connections and urban development.

Environmental Impact

By providing efficient public transportation, the Green Line plays a crucial role in reducing carbon emissions and promoting sustainable urban mobility in the Greater Boston area.
